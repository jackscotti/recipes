# Quick Tantanmen (Sesame Chili Ramen)

The soupy, creamy cousin of mapo tofu: a rich sesame-and-chili broth with a
spicy pork crumble over ramen. A weeknight shortcut version, about 20 minutes.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes
**Cook time:** 15 minutes
**Servings:** 2

---

## Ingredients

**Spicy pork**
- 200g ground pork
- 1 tbsp doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste)
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tsp grated ginger
- 1 tbsp soy sauce

**Broth**
- 3 tbsp Asian sesame paste (or tahini)
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tbsp chili oil, plus extra to serve
- 500ml chicken or vegetable stock, hot

**To serve**
- 2 portions ramen noodles
- 2 baby pak choi, halved
- 2 scallions, sliced

---

## Method

1. Fry the pork in a dry pan over high heat until browned. Add the doubanjiang,
   garlic, and ginger and cook 2 minutes, then deglaze with the soy sauce. Set
   aside.

2. Divide the sesame paste, soy sauce, sugar, and chili oil between two bowls and
   whisk in the hot stock until smooth and creamy.

3. Cook the noodles per the package instructions, blanching the pak choi in the
   same water for the last minute. Drain.

4. Divide the noodles and greens between the bowls, top with the spicy pork and
   scallions, and add extra chili oil to taste.

---

## Notes

- Swap pork for ground chicken, turkey, or a plant-based mince.
- Sesame paste gives the signature creaminess — natural peanut butter works in a
  pinch.
