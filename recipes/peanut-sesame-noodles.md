# Peanut Sesame Noodles

A fast, creamy peanut-sauce noodle bowl that works hot or cold. No real cooking
beyond boiling the noodles — about 15 minutes.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes
**Cook time:** 5 minutes
**Servings:** 2

---

## Ingredients

**Peanut sauce**
- 3 tbsp smooth peanut butter
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp rice vinegar
- 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
- 1 tsp sugar or honey
- 1 tbsp chili crisp, plus extra to serve
- 1 clove garlic, grated
- Warm water, to loosen

**To serve**
- 2 portions noodles (udon, ramen, or wheat noodles)
- 1 carrot, julienned
- 1/2 cucumber, julienned
- 2 scallions, sliced
- Cooked shredded chicken or crispy tofu (optional)
- Crushed peanuts, to garnish

---

## Method

1. Whisk together all the sauce ingredients, adding warm water a tablespoon at a
   time until it's a pourable, creamy consistency.

2. Cook the noodles per the package instructions. Drain and, if serving cold,
   rinse under cold water.

3. Toss the noodles with the sauce. Top with the carrot, cucumber, scallions,
   and chicken or tofu if using.

4. Finish with crushed peanuts and extra chili crisp.

---

## Notes

- Great for meal prep — keep the sauce and noodles separate and combine when
  serving.
- Tahini can stand in for peanut butter for a nuttier, less sweet version.
