# Creamy Mapo Tofu Udon

A viral, high-protein weeknight noodle bowl: chewy udon draped in a smooth
blended silken-tofu sauce, topped with spicy mapo-style ground beef. Comes
together in about 20 minutes.

Source: https://www.kitchenstories.com/en/recipes/creamy-mapo-tofu-udon

**Prep time:** 5 minutes
**Cook time:** 15 minutes
**Servings:** 2

---

## Ingredients

**Creamy tofu sauce**
- 300g silken tofu
- 1 tbsp miso paste
- Pinch of salt

**Mapo beef**
- 250g ground beef
- 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- 2 scallions
- 1–2 tbsp doubanjiang (fermented chili bean paste)
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil (for frying)

**To serve**
- 2 portions udon noodles (approx. 400g fresh / 200g dried)
- Chili crisp or chili oil, to taste

---

## Utensils

Blender or immersion blender, pot, frying pan, cutting board, knife.

---

## Method

1. Add the silken tofu, miso, and a pinch of salt to a container and blend
   until completely smooth and creamy. Set aside.

2. Peel and finely chop the garlic. Cut the scallions into fine rings, setting
   aside some of the green parts for garnish.

3. Bring a pot of water to the boil and cook the udon noodles according to the
   package instructions. Drain.

4. Meanwhile, heat the oil in a pan and fry the garlic and scallions until
   translucent. Add the doubanjiang and fry for about 2 minutes.

5. Add the ground beef and fry until thoroughly cooked, approximately 8 minutes.
   Deglaze with the soy sauce and set aside.

6. Plate the udon, pour the creamy tofu over the top, then spoon over the spicy
   ground beef. Garnish with the reserved scallion greens and a drizzle of chili
   crisp before serving.

---

## Notes

- **Protein swaps:** Ground pork, ground chicken, or a plant-based mince all
  work in place of beef.
- **Heat level:** Doubanjiang is the traditional paste; gochujang can be used as
  a milder, slightly sweeter substitute. Adjust the amount to taste.
- Quantities follow the standard Kitchen Stories version; season the sauce and
  chili to your own taste.
