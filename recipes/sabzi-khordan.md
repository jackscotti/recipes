# Sabzi Khordan (Persian Herb & Feta Plate)

The fresh herb platter that anchors every Persian table — a generous pile of
soft herbs, feta, walnuts, and crisp radishes, eaten by tearing off flatbread
and building little bites between mouthfuls of grilled meat. No cooking, just
washing and arranging.

**Prep time:** 15 minutes
**Cook time:** 0 minutes
**Servings:** 4
**Cuisine:** Persian
**Meal type:** Side
**Dietary:** Vegetarian
**Cooking method:** No-cook

---

## Ingredients

- 1 large bunch fresh mint
- 1 large bunch fresh basil (or Persian basil / reyhan)
- 1 large bunch flat-leaf parsley
- 1 small bunch tarragon
- 1 small bunch chives or spring onions
- 200g feta, cut into slabs or cubes
- 100g walnut halves
- 1 bunch radishes, trimmed (halved if large)
- Shortcut sangak or other flatbread, to serve (see `shortcut-sangak.md`)

---

## Method

1. Wash all the herbs well and spin or pat them thoroughly dry — any water left
   clinging makes the plate soggy and dilutes the flavour.

2. Pick the herbs into loose sprigs, discarding thick or woody stems. Keep them
   in generous tufts rather than chopping.

3. Arrange the herbs across a large platter in loose bunches.

4. Nestle the feta, walnuts, and radishes among the herbs.

5. Serve at room temperature alongside warm flatbread. To eat, tear off a piece
   of bread, add feta, a few herbs, a walnut, and a radish, and fold into a
   bite.

---

## Notes

- Dryness is everything — damp herbs wilt fast and turn the plate limp. Wash
  well ahead and dry thoroughly.
- Soak the walnuts in cold water for 15 minutes for a softer, less bitter bite,
  a common Persian touch.
- Use whatever soft herbs you can get; dill, cilantro, and summer savory all
  belong here. Avoid tough, woody herbs like rosemary or thyme.
- A wedge of the feta with a walnut and a mint leaf, wrapped in warm sangak, is
  the classic mouthful — build it alongside the lamb.
