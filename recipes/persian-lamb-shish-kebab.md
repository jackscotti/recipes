# Persian Lamb Shish Kebab

Cubes of lamb marinated in grated onion, garlic, saffron, and lemon, then
grilled hot and fast until charred outside and juicy within. Built to be torn
into warm sangak and eaten with herbs and yogurt. Marinate ahead — the active
work is minimal.

**Prep time:** 20 minutes (plus 4 hours marinating)
**Cook time:** 10 minutes
**Servings:** 4
**Cuisine:** Persian
**Meal type:** Dinner
**Dietary:** Halal-friendly
**Cooking method:** Grill

---

## Ingredients

**Lamb & marinade**
- 800g boneless lamb leg or shoulder, cut into 3-4cm cubes
- 1 large onion, grated (with juices)
- 3 garlic cloves, crushed
- 1/4 tsp saffron threads, bloomed in 2 tbsp warm water
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Juice of 1 lemon
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp ground cumin

**To serve**
- Shortcut sangak (see `shortcut-sangak.md`)
- Grilled tomatoes and onions
- Sumac, for sprinkling
- Fresh herbs (parsley, mint, basil)
- Lemon wedges
- Plain yogurt or mast-o-khiar

---

## Method

1. In a large bowl, combine the grated onion and its juices, garlic, bloomed
   saffron, olive oil, lemon juice, salt, pepper, and cumin.

2. Add the lamb cubes and turn to coat thoroughly. Cover and marinate in the
   fridge for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight. Bring to room temperature
   before cooking.

3. Thread the lamb onto skewers, packing the cubes snugly but not too tight.
   If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes first.

4. Heat a grill, griddle pan, or barbecue to high. Grill the kebabs for 8-10
   minutes total, turning every couple of minutes, until charred outside and
   just pink within.

5. Rest the kebabs for 5 minutes, then sprinkle with sumac.

6. Serve on warm sangak with grilled tomatoes and onions, fresh herbs, lemon
   wedges, and yogurt. Tear the bread, wrap the lamb, and eat.

---

## Notes

- The grated onion is the key tenderiser — don't skip its juices, they carry
  most of the flavour into the meat.
- High heat and quick turning give char without drying the lamb; avoid low-and-
  slow, which stews the cubes.
- Lamb shoulder has more fat and stays juicier; leg is leaner and neater on the
  skewer.
- Wrap the grilled kebabs in the warm sangak straight off the heat and let them
  steam together for a minute before serving.
- **No grill? Any high, dry heat works.** A screaming-hot cast iron pan gives the
  closest char (cook in batches, don't crowd, and cook the cubes loose off the
  skewers); an air fryer at 200°C for ~10-12 minutes is the easiest and cleanest,
  turning halfway, though with a little less char; or the oven grill/broiler on
  its highest setting with the meat on a rack near the element.
- **Only bone-in lamb available?** Ask the butcher to cube it bone-in (buy ~1kg
  to yield ~800g of meat), cook the pieces loose rather than skewered, and give
  them a few extra minutes at slightly gentler heat so the meat near the bone
  cooks through.
- **No boneless cut at all?** Switch to koobideh (see `persian-koobideh.md`) —
  minced lamb needs no deboning and no long marinade, and suits the same sangak,
  herbs, and yogurt spread.
