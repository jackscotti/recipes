# Persian Koobideh (Minced Lamb Kebab)

Minced lamb kneaded with grated onion and saffron, shaped into flat logs and
grilled hard and fast. The no-fuss cousin of shish kebab — no boneless cut to
source, no long marinade, just mix, rest, and cook. Built to be torn into warm
sangak with herbs and yogurt.

**Prep time:** 15 minutes (plus 30 minutes resting)
**Cook time:** 8 minutes
**Servings:** 4
**Cuisine:** Persian
**Meal type:** Dinner
**Dietary:** Halal-friendly
**Cooking method:** Grill

---

## Ingredients

- 800g lamb mince (ideally ~20% fat, so it stays juicy)
- 1 large onion, grated and squeezed very dry
- 1/4 tsp saffron threads, bloomed in 2 tbsp warm water
- 1 tsp salt
- 1/2 tsp black pepper
- 1/2 tsp ground cumin (optional)

**To serve**
- Shortcut sangak (see `shortcut-sangak.md`)
- Grilled tomatoes and onions
- Fresh herbs / sabzi khordan (see `sabzi-khordan.md`)
- Lemon wedges
- Plain yogurt or mast-o-khiar

---

## Method

1. Crush the saffron and steep in 2 tbsp warm water for 5-10 minutes, until deep
   gold.

2. Grate the onion, then squeeze it hard (in your hands or a clean cloth) to
   remove as much liquid as possible. Wet onion makes the mince too loose to
   hold its shape.

3. Combine the mince, squeezed onion, saffron water, salt, pepper, and cumin.
   Knead firmly for 3-5 minutes, until smooth, sticky, and paste-like — this is
   what binds the kebabs.

4. Cover and chill for at least 30 minutes (longer is fine). The mince firms up
   and holds its shape better when cold.

5. Shape into flat logs, either pressed onto flat skewers or formed by hand.
   Cook over high heat — cast iron, barbecue, or a hot pan — for 3-4 minutes a
   side, until charred outside and cooked through.

6. Rest for a couple of minutes, squeeze over lemon, and serve on warm sangak
   with grilled tomatoes, herbs, and yogurt.

---

## Notes

- **Squeezing the onion dry is the make-or-break step** — excess water is the
  main reason koobideh falls apart on the heat.
- **Knead well.** The mince should turn tacky and paste-like; under-kneaded meat
  won't hold together.
- **Rest cold, shape late.** Chill the mince before shaping, and shape it just
  before cooking — shaped raw logs left to sit will slump.
- **Fattier mince is better** — lean mince dries out over high heat.
- A great swap when the butcher has no boneless cut for shish kebab: mince needs
  no deboning and no long marinade.
- **Want more spice?** The base seasoning is deliberately restrained. For more
  depth, knead in 1/2 tsp turmeric, a crushed garlic clove or two, and 1 tsp
  dried mint. For a fuller flavour, add ~1 1/2 tsp advieh (Persian spice blend):
  1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, 1/2 tsp cinnamon, and 1/4 tsp cardamom, plus a
  pinch of dried rose petals if you have them. A pinch of chilli flakes or
  cayenne adds heat. Mix any extra spices in at the kneading stage so they
  distribute evenly.
