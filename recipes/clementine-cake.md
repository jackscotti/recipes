# Low Sugar Clementine Cake

*A moist, naturally sweet clementine cake using almond flour and Greek yoghurt in place of excess sugar.*

**Serves:** 8

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Amount |
|---|---|
| Clementines (whole, washed, stems trimmed) | 240 g |
| Almond flour | 200 g |
| Eggs | 4 |
| Greek yoghurt | 150 g |
| Sugar | 60 g |
| Unsalted butter, melted and cooled | 50 g |
| Baking powder | 1 tsp |
| Salt | 1 pinch |

---

## Method

1. **Boil the clementines** — Place the clementines in a saucepan, cover with cold water and bring to the boil. Simmer for 90 minutes until completely soft. Drain and leave to cool.

2. **Preheat oven** — Preheat your oven to 160°C (fan). Grease and line a 20cm round cake tin.

3. **Blitz the clementines** — Once cooled, cut the clementines in half and remove any obvious pips. Blitz the entire fruit (skin, pith and all) in a food processor until you have a smooth purée.

4. **Mix the batter** — In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and sugar until combined. Add the Greek yoghurt, melted butter, almond flour, baking powder, and salt, and mix well. Fold in the clementine purée until everything is evenly combined.

5. **Bake** — Pour the batter into your prepared tin and bake for 50-60 minutes until golden and a skewer inserted into the centre comes out clean. Cover loosely with foil after 30 minutes if the top is browning too quickly.

6. **Cool and serve** — Leave to cool in the tin for 15 minutes before turning out onto a wire rack. Dust with a little icing sugar or drizzle with honey to serve if you'd like a touch more sweetness.

---

## Notes

The cake keeps well for 2-3 days in an airtight container and actually improves the next day as the flavours develop. It also freezes well. If your clementines are particularly sweet you could reduce the sugar to 40g or even skip it entirely.

Remember to melt the butter and let it cool slightly before mixing it in so it doesn't scramble the eggs.
