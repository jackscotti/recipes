# Shoyu Mazemen (Brothless Mix Noodles)

Brothless ramen: noodles tossed with a concentrated soy tare, chili oil, and a
runny egg yolk, then mixed at the table. Endlessly customisable, about 15
minutes.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes
**Cook time:** 10 minutes
**Servings:** 2

---

## Ingredients

**Tare (per bowl, x2)**
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp oyster sauce
- 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
- 1 tsp chili oil
- 1/2 tsp sugar

**To serve**
- 2 portions ramen noodles
- 2 egg yolks (or soft-boiled eggs)
- 2 scallions, finely sliced
- Nori, cut into strips
- Toasted sesame seeds
- Optional toppings: crispy pork, corn, menma, chili crisp

---

## Method

1. In the bottom of each serving bowl, mix the soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame
   oil, chili oil, and sugar.

2. Cook the noodles per the package instructions until just done. Drain well —
   the noodles should be barely wet.

3. Add the hot noodles to each bowl and toss thoroughly to coat in the tare.

4. Top with an egg yolk, scallions, nori, sesame seeds, and any extra toppings.
   Mix everything together well before eating.

---

## Notes

- The key is well-drained noodles and a punchy, concentrated tare — there's no
  broth to carry the seasoning.
- A splash of reserved noodle water helps everything cling together.
