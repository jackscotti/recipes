# Yaki Udon (Stir-Fried Udon)

The fast weeknight workhorse: thick udon stir-fried with protein and vegetables
in a savoury soy-mirin sauce. About 15 minutes.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes
**Cook time:** 10 minutes
**Servings:** 2

---

## Ingredients

**Sauce**
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp oyster sauce
- 1 tbsp mirin
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 tsp Worcestershire sauce (optional)

**Stir-fry**
- 2 portions udon noodles (pre-cooked or fresh)
- 150g protein (thinly sliced pork, chicken, or prawns)
- 1 tbsp neutral oil
- 1/2 onion, sliced
- 1 carrot, julienned
- 2 handfuls cabbage, shredded
- 2 scallions, cut into batons
- To garnish: bonito flakes, pickled ginger (optional)

---

## Method

1. Stir the sauce ingredients together in a small bowl.

2. If using fresh or frozen udon, loosen it in hot water for a minute, then
   drain.

3. Heat the oil in a wok or large pan over high heat. Stir-fry the protein until
   almost cooked, then add the onion, carrot, and cabbage and stir-fry 2–3
   minutes until crisp-tender.

4. Add the udon and the sauce and toss over high heat until everything is coated
   and glossy. Stir through the scallions.

5. Serve topped with bonito flakes and pickled ginger if you like.

---

## Notes

- Endlessly flexible — use whatever protein and vegetables you have on hand.
- Keep the heat high and the noodles moving so they char slightly rather than
  steam.
