# Dan Dan Noodles

The Sichuan classic that mapo tofu udon borrows from: a nutty, tingly sesame-chili
sauce under a crisp, savoury pork-and-preserved-vegetable topping. About 25
minutes.

**Prep time:** 10 minutes
**Cook time:** 15 minutes
**Servings:** 2

---

## Ingredients

**Sauce (divided between 2 bowls)**
- 2 tbsp Chinese sesame paste (or tahini)
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 2 tbsp chili oil, with sediment
- 1/2 tsp ground toasted Sichuan pepper
- 1 clove garlic, grated
- 1 tsp sugar
- Splash of noodle water

**Pork topping**
- 200g ground pork
- 2 tbsp ya cai (preserved mustard greens), rinsed and chopped
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp Shaoxing wine

**To serve**
- 2 portions thin wheat noodles
- 2 scallions, sliced
- Handful of blanched leafy greens (optional)

---

## Method

1. Fry the pork in a little oil over high heat until dry and crisp. Add the ya
   cai, soy sauce, and Shaoxing wine and cook 2 minutes more. Set aside.

2. Divide the sauce ingredients between two bowls and stir in a splash of hot
   noodle water to loosen into a pourable sauce.

3. Cook the noodles per the package instructions, blanching any greens in the
   same water. Drain.

4. Add the noodles to the bowls, top with the crispy pork and scallions, and
   toss everything together at the table before eating.

---

## Notes

- Ya cai (Sichuan preserved mustard greens) gives the authentic savoury edge; if
  unavailable, leave it out or use a little more soy.
- The toasted Sichuan pepper is what makes it "málà" — tingly and numbing.
Adjust to taste.
