# Lemon Currant Cake Recipe

*Metric · Greek yogurt · Reduced sugar*

## Ingredients

- **360ml Greek yogurt** (plain) — about 400–430g from a 1kg tub
- **200g sugar**
- **6 large eggs**
- **375g all-purpose flour**
- **4g baking soda** (or 12g baking powder — see notes)
- **15ml lemon juice** (only if using baking soda)
- **5g salt**
- **30ml grated lemon zest** (or zest of 2 large lemons)
- **300g fresh red currants**

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease and flour a bundt pan or large cake pan.

2. In a large bowl, whisk together the Greek yogurt and sugar until well combined.

3. Beat in eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.

4. In a separate bowl, whisk together flour, baking soda (or baking powder), and salt.

5. Gradually fold the dry ingredients into the wet ingredients until just combined. If using baking soda, add the lemon juice now and work quickly. Don't overmix.

6. Fold in the lemon zest and fresh red currants gently.

7. Pour batter into prepared pan and bake for 50–60 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted in the centre comes out clean.

8. Cool in pan for 10 minutes before turning out onto a wire rack.

## Notes

- **Leavening:** Use either 4g baking soda + 15ml lemon juice, or 12g baking powder. The baking soda route gives a slightly more tender crumb and a touch more lemon flavour; baking powder gives a more reliable rise.
- **Greek yogurt** replaces both the original whole-milk yogurt and the oil, making the cake denser, moister, and more protein-rich.
- **Reduced sugar** (200g instead of 400g) lets the tartness of the lemon and currants come through. Work quickly once leavening is mixed in.
- **Red currants:** fresh or frozen both work — if frozen, don't thaw first.

## Quick conversion reference

| Ingredient | Volume | Weight |
|---|---|---|
| Baking powder | 20ml | 12g |
| Baking soda | 5ml | 4g |
| Salt | 2.5ml | 5g |
| Greek yogurt | 360ml | ~400–430g |
