# Blueberry Cake (Butter Version)

A lemon-blueberry springform cake made with butter and Greek yoghurt. Dry and solid ingredients are in grams; liquids are in millilitres.

**Servings:** 1 x 22cm cake (approx. 10 slices)
**Prep time:** 15 minutes
**Bake time:** 50 minutes

## Ingredients

### Blueberries
- 375g blueberries, fresh, divided (3 punnets) — see Note 1 for frozen
- 15g plain flour (all purpose)

### Wet
- 275g white sugar, preferably caster / superfine
- 150g butter, melted and slightly cooled
- 2 eggs
- 1 ½ tbsp grated lemon rind
- 65ml lemon juice
- 250g plain Greek yoghurt

### Dry
- 335g plain flour (all purpose)
- 20g baking powder — see Note 2 for bicarbonate of soda
- Pinch of salt

## Instructions

1. Preheat oven to 200°C (standard) or 180°C (fan / convection). Grease a 22cm springform pan with butter and line the base with baking paper.
2. Rinse the blueberries and shake off excess water, but do not pat dry. Set aside about 1/3 of the blueberries for topping the cake. Toss the remaining blueberries in the 15g of flour and set aside.
3. Whisk all wet ingredients together in a large bowl. Add the 335g flour and salt, then sprinkle the baking powder over the top.
4. Whisk until just combined; a few small lumps are fine. Stir through the floured blueberries, including any loose flour in the bowl. Pour into the pan and top with the reserved blueberries.
5. Place in the oven and TURN DOWN to 180°C (standard) or 160°C (fan / convection). Bake for 50 minutes, or until a skewer comes out clean. Remove the sides of the springform pan and cool on a rack, then remove the base once fully cool.
6. Sprinkle with icing sugar and serve.

## Notes

- **Note 1 (frozen blueberries):** Do not thaw first. Use them straight from frozen.
- **Note 2 (raising agent):** To substitute bicarbonate of soda, use 5g instead of the 20g baking powder.
- **Butter tip:** Let the melted butter cool slightly before mixing it with the other wet ingredients, otherwise it may begin to cook the eggs.
