# Ooni Classic Pizza Dough

Neapolitan-style dough (flour, water, salt, yeast) for a puffy, foldable crust.
60% hydration, slow same-day ferment (3-6 hours). Uses instant dried yeast and
makes 16" pizzas.

Source: https://ooni.com/blogs/recipes/ooni-classic-pizza-dough

**Makes: 6 × 16" pizzas (350g dough balls).**

## Base recipe — 6 pizzas (350g each, 16")

| Ingredient            | Amount  |
|-----------------------|---------|
| 00 flour              | 1344g   |
| Lukewarm water        | 800g    |
| Salt                  | 27g     |
| Instant dried yeast   | 3g      |

Total dough: ~2144g. Hydration 60%, salt 2%.

To scale to a different number of 16" balls, multiply each ingredient by
(number of pizzas / 6) — e.g. 5 pizzas = × 5/6.

## Method (mixer)

1. Combine the water, salt, and yeast in a vessel; stir until dissolved.
2. Add the flour to the mixer bowl.
3. Mix at low speed (~25% / 120 rpm) and pour in the liquid. Mix to a shaggy
   dough, then stop.
4. Cover and rest 10 minutes.
5. Mix again at low speed (~15% / 90 rpm) for ~30 seconds to develop the gluten.
6. Remove the bowl, cover with a damp towel, and let rise in a warm spot ~2
   hours, until doubled.
7. Divide into 350g balls.
8. Flour the dough boxes, place a ball in each, and proof 30-60 minutes until
   doubled.
9. Heat the oven to 400-450°C and bake each pizza 1-2 minutes.

## Yeast notes

Source lists "3g yeast (fresh or instant)" — using instant dried, that's **3g**,
mixed straight into the liquid (no separate activation needed). This is a low
dose for a slow ferment, so don't bump it up unless you're shortening the rise.

For Idun (Norwegian) instant dried yeast: 1 tsp ≈ 3g, so the base batch is about
1 level teaspoon.
