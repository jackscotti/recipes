# Garlic Chili Oil Noodles (You Po Mian)

The fastest noodle fix there is: aromatics topped with sizzling hot oil, then
tossed with soy and vinegar. About 10 minutes, almost no shopping.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes
**Cook time:** 5 minutes
**Servings:** 2

---

## Ingredients

- 2 portions wheat noodles (or udon)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 scallions, finely sliced
- 2 tbsp chili flakes (Sichuan or Korean gochugaru)
- 1 tsp toasted sesame seeds
- 4 tbsp neutral oil
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp black rice vinegar
- 1 tsp sugar

---

## Method

1. Cook the noodles per the package instructions, reserving a splash of the
   water. Drain and divide between two bowls.

2. To each bowl add half the garlic, scallions, chili flakes, and sesame seeds,
   piling the aromatics on top of the noodles.

3. Heat the oil until shimmering and just smoking, then pour it directly over the
   aromatics — it should sizzle. Stand back.

4. Add the soy sauce, vinegar, and sugar, plus a splash of noodle water, and toss
   everything together well.

---

## Notes

- Get the oil hot enough that it audibly sizzles on contact — that's what blooms
  the garlic and chili.
- Add a fried egg or blanched greens to make it more of a meal.
