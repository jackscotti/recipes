# Gochujang Butter Noodles

The natural next step from creamy mapo udon: chewy noodles tossed in a glossy,
spicy-savoury emulsion of gochujang and butter. Ready in about 15 minutes.

**Prep time:** 5 minutes
**Cook time:** 10 minutes
**Servings:** 2

---

## Ingredients

- 2 portions udon or ramen noodles
- 2 tbsp gochujang
- 30g butter
- 1 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp sugar or honey
- 2 cloves garlic, finely grated
- 1 tsp toasted sesame oil
- 1 scallion, sliced
- Optional topping: fried egg, or ground pork fried until crisp
- To garnish: toasted sesame seeds, extra scallion

---

## Method

1. Cook the noodles according to the package instructions. Reserve about 100ml
   of the cooking water before draining.

2. In a wide pan over medium heat, melt the butter and gently fry the garlic for
   30 seconds until fragrant. Stir in the gochujang, soy sauce, and sugar and
   cook for 1 minute.

3. Add a splash of the reserved noodle water and stir into a smooth, glossy
   sauce, loosening with more water as needed.

4. Add the drained noodles and toss to coat. Finish with the sesame oil and
   scallion.

5. Serve topped with a fried egg or crispy pork, and a scatter of sesame seeds.

---

## Notes

- Uses the same doubanjiang/gochujang and udon you'd keep for mapo tofu udon.
- For extra richness, whisk an egg yolk into the hot noodles off the heat.
